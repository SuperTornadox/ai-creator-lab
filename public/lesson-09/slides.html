What Are Deepfakes? 🎭

Deepfakes are images, videos, or audio created by AI that look and sound incredibly real — but are completely fake.
📸
Fake Photos
People who never existed, places that aren't real
🎥
Fake Videos
Making someone appear to say things they never said
🎙️
Fake Audio
Cloning someone's voice to say anything

Spotting Fake Images 🖼️

AI images look real, but they leave clues:

  • 🖐️ Hands & fingers — extra fingers, weird bending
  • 👀 Eyes — different sizes, strange reflections
  • 📝 Text in images — jumbled letters, nonsense words
  • ✨ Too perfect — impossibly smooth skin, no flaws
  • 🖼️ Backgrounds — blurry, melting, or impossible shapes

Spotting AI Writing ✍️

🧑 Human Writing

- Personal stories & messy details
- Casual language, slang
- Strong opinions & emotions
- Small mistakes, typos
- Unique voice & personality

🤖 AI Writing

- General facts, no personal touch
- Formal, polished language
- Balanced, avoids controversy
- Perfect grammar always
- Sounds like a textbook

AI text is correct but often boring.

AI Deception in the Real World ⚠️

This is already happening:

📰
Fake News Articles
AI writes entire news stories that never happened, and people share them
📱
Social Media Fakes
AI-generated photos of fake people with thousands of followers
📞
Voice Cloning Scams
Criminals clone a family member's voice to trick people into sending money

This is why learning to spot fakes matters!

What Is AI Bias? ⚖️

Bias means unfairly favoring or disfavoring certain groups of people.

AI can learn bias from the data it's trained on — if the training data contains stereotypes, the AI will repeat them!

Example: Ask AI to "describe a CEO" — does it always imagine a man?

Where Does Bias Come From? 📚

📚 Training Data
billions of texts
written by people
→
🤖 AI Learns
patterns & associations
(including stereotypes)
→
💬 AI Output
may repeat unfair
assumptions
If most books & websites describe nurses as women and engineers as men, the AI might do the same — even though anyone can be either!

Bias in Action: Real Examples 🚨

🏢
Hiring Software
An AI hiring tool rejected women's resumes because it learned from past (biased) hiring data
🔎
Search Results
Searching "doctor" mostly showed men; searching "nurse" mostly showed women
🏥
Healthcare
An AI system gave less care to certain patients based on their background

AI bias is not just annoying — it can cause real harm to real people.

5 Principles for Responsible AI Use 💡

  • 🔍 Verify — Don't believe everything; check the source
  • ⚖️ Question bias — AI can be unfair; think critically
  • 🔒 Protect privacy — Never share personal info with AI
  • 🎨 Give credit — Be honest about AI help in your work
  • 🛡️ Stay safe — Tell an adult if AI does something wrong
